TITLE: "THE DEEP HORIZON"
GENRE: Sci-Fi Thriller
WRITER: Jordan Chen (fictional, first-time produced credit)
DRAFT: Production Treatment, June 2026 — 110 pages

LOGLINE
When a deep-sea research station discovers an alien signal emanating from the
Mariana Trench, a team of scientists must descend to the ocean floor before a
rival military operation seals the site — but the signal is changing everyone
who hears it.

===========================================================
TREATMENT SUMMARY
===========================================================

ACT 1 (pp 1-30) — SURFACE
-----------------------------------------------------------
INT. NOAA HEADQUARTERS, WASHINGTON DC — DAY
Establishing montage. Director MIRA HASSAN (40s, lead) briefs the
inter-agency team. Introduces the alien signal. (4 speaking roles)

EXT. PACIFIC OCEAN, AT SEA — DAY (MARINE UNIT)
The research vessel "Horizon" rendezvouses with the descent submersible.
Helicopter inserts equipment. Drone footage establishes scale.

INT. RESEARCH VESSEL HORIZON — VARIOUS
Lab scenes, crew introductions: DR. ELIAS PARK (40s, lead),
COMMANDER REYES (50s, lead), JUNIOR TECH AARON (20s, supporting).
Five additional supporting roles (vessel crew). Eight day players (boat
hands, comms staff, briefing extras).

Total Act 1 VFX: ~5 shots (drone establishing, signal visualization, sonar UI)
Total Act 1 locations: 4 interior, 2 exterior (sea)

ACT 2 (pp 31-85) — DESCENT
-----------------------------------------------------------
EXT. UNDERWATER, DEEP OCEAN — VARIOUS (UNDERWATER UNIT + CG)
Multiple deep-sea diving sequences. Submersible POV plates.
Practical underwater photography combined with CG environments.
At least 15 dedicated VFX shots across this stretch.

INT. DEEP-SEA STATION (PRACTICAL SET + VFX EXTENSIONS)
The team boards an abandoned research station 11km down. Approximately
4,000 sq ft practical set build is required. Window views are full CG
ocean environments. 20+ VFX shots for window extensions, instrument
panels, holographic displays.

EXT. UNDERWATER — ALIEN BIOLUMINESCENT ENVIRONMENT (FULL CG)
The signal source — a vast bioluminescent cavern. Full CG environment.
10+ VFX shots, photoreal water lighting required.

INT. MILITARY SUBMARINE — 3 SCENES
The rival military operation, led by ADMIRAL VOSS (50s, supporting).
Three scenes; could be practical set or VFX extension depending on
production design.

NIGHT SHOOTS — Act 2 is approximately 40% nighttime / interior-dark
to maintain the deep-sea aesthetic.

ACT 3 (pp 86-110) — RESURFACE
-----------------------------------------------------------
EXT. UNDERWATER — STATION COLLAPSE FINALE
Complex VFX action climax: imploding station, debris field, structural
collapse practical-CG hybrid. 10+ VFX shots.

EXT. OCEAN SURFACE — DAY (MARINE + AERIAL UNIT)
Rescue sequence: surfacing submersible, helicopter extraction,
ocean-skimming pursuit. Requires marine unit + aerial unit.

INT. HOSPITAL, US WEST COAST
Denouement. Two scenes. The signal echoes through the comatose survivors.

- Practical-CG hybrid finale (station collapse)

WRITER NOTE:
This is a tight, character-driven thriller in the tradition of
"The Abyss" (1989) and "Sphere" (1998), updated for contemporary
underwater VFX techniques. Three leads carry the emotional throughline;
the alien signal acts as a unifying mystery rather than a creature
antagonist (the threat is internal and psychological).
